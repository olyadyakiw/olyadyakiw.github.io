Soup Magazine

  • Print
  • Buy one

Desktop publishing became a viable means of disseminating new ideas and images around 1990. Towards the end of graduate school, I edited and published a semi-regular zine called Soup Magazine that combined the work of professional artists with that of emerging ones.

At that time, it wasn’t so hard to get established artists like Robert Longo, Barbara Ess and Vito Acconci to participate in our project. This was an entirely self-funded effort and the magazine was sold at A/C Project Room (New York), Printed Matter (New York) and Tower Books (throughout the United States). I think it might still be at Printer Matter.

1996

Andrew is a designer, artist and creative director based in Winnipeg, Treaty One Territory, Canada. I can be reached electronically at andrew@manoverboard.com.
